99% of Those Who Died From Virus
Had Other Illness, Italy Says

By Tommaso Ebharat, Chiara Remondini, and Marco Bertacche
March 18, 2020, 8:56 AM EDT

More than 99% of Italy’s coronavirus fatalities were people who suffered from
previous medical conditions, according to a study by the country’s national
health authority.

After deaths from the virus reached more than 2,500, with a 150% increase in
the past week, health authorities have been combing through data to provide
clues to help combat the spread of the disease.
